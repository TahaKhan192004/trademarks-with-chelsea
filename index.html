Meet Chelsea

The lawyer who's also been the business owner.

I started practicing law in 2008, working mostly with business owners. It must have rubbed off on me, because here's what else I've been up to since:

  • Coaching business owners, and growing into a decade-long consulting practice
  • Joining the founding team of a brick-and-mortar startup (and winning a business plan competition along the way)
  • Co-founding a small business law firm, and later an online marketing agency

Every one of those paths came back to the same thing: helping business owners build something that holds up.

Now I'm back, practicing exclusively trademark law. If I've learned anything from being in the trenches as an entrepreneur, it's that what you build has to be protected in order to be sustainable.

Brand protection matters more than ever as AI changes how fast a name, a brand, or an idea can be copied or confused.

Wherever you are in growing a brand, your name is on the line.

A lot of my clients are experts who wouldn't necessarily call themselves one. Amazing at what they do, and looking to grow a sustainable business of impact and value. If this sounds like you, part of that future growth should include a trademark strategy.

One continuous path, from idea to protected.

Most clients come to me before they've filed anything; some find me mid-process, in need of someone to take it the rest of the way. Either way, the work follows the same arc.

  1. 01

    Strategy Consultation

    We talk through what you're building and what's worth protecting.

  2. 02

    Clearance Search

    A thorough search confirms the path is actually open before you invest further.

  3. 03

    Application

    Your application is prepared and filed with the USPTO, done right the first time.

  4. 04

    Registration

    Once the USPTO approves it, your mark is federally registered.

  5. 05

    Maintenance

    The relationship doesn't end. A trademark has to be maintained and renewed to stay protected, and I stay on as the one watching out for your business.
